# Senior SWE Call Notes

## Positioning

I’m looking for a strong senior-level fit where I can contribute in product engineering, full-stack/frontend work, architecture, and helping teams move faster.

I’m not desperate for a job, so fit + scope + compensation should align.

---

## Rate

Target: **$90/h**  
Private minimum: **$70/h** — do **not** mention first.

### If they ask my rate

> For senior software engineering contract work, I’m generally targeting around **$90/hour**, depending on scope, duration, expectations, and whether this is purely contract or contract-to-hire.

### If role is still vague

> I can share a rate, but I’d prefer to understand the role a bit first so I don’t give a number without context.

### If they push back

> What range did you have budgeted for this role?

Then:

> I’m open to discussing structure depending on contract length, expectations, and conversion path. For a senior role with broad ownership, I’d be more comfortable closer to **$85–90/h**.

---

## Contractor vs Employee

Contract rate should be higher than salary-equivalent because contractor usually has:

- no paid vacation
- no sick days
- no benefits
- no severance
- less stability
- possible unpaid gaps
- more tax/admin risk

If contract-to-hire:

> I’m open to contract-to-hire if there’s a clear path and mutual fit. For the contract phase, I’d still price it as contract work. If we later discuss direct hire, we can evaluate salary, benefits, equity, and the full package separately.

---

## Questions to Ask

### Role

- What problem are you hiring this person to solve?
- What does success look like in the first 30/60/90 days?
- Is this execution-heavy, architecture-heavy, or product ownership-heavy?
- Would I join an existing team or work independently?
- What level of seniority/ownership do you expect?

### Contract

- Is this short-term, long-term, or contract-to-hire?
- Expected duration?
- Full-time 40h/week or flexible hours?
- Any timezone/availability expectations?
- Is there a budgeted hourly range?

### Technical

- What stack are you using?
- Current team size/composition?
- Biggest technical challenges right now?
- Existing codebase maturity: early-stage, scaling, legacy, refactor?

---

## Good Signals

- Clear problem
- Clear budget
- Senior ownership
- Good team/process
- Long enough contract
- Path to conversion, but not used to lower rate
- Product-focused engineering

## Caution Signals

- Vague scope
- No budget clarity
- “Possible conversion” used to justify low rate
- Senior expectations at mid-level rate
- Very short contract with heavy ownership
- No clear decision-maker

---

## Closing Line

> This sounds potentially aligned. Based on what I heard, I think the next step would be to clarify scope, expected timeline, and budget range so we can make sure it works for both sides.
